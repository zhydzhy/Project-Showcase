I had been looking for a fulltime job since September of 2025, this website was the byproduct of that quest.
I needed to show the world what I had done and what I'm capable of, but technology had been evolving so fast,
from just copy-pasting html and css code, to now I just prompt codex what type of design I would want for this 
website. 

It is clear to me though, the engineering mindset is even more valuable than in the past. I had been telling my
coworkers while I was interning at Boeing Tianjin, that in the future (possibly a very near one), coding skill will
just be like English. With business needs, English is a great tool, but English by itself is non-monetizable. The need of human connections between customers and engineers is becoming more and more prominent.

I think people with a CS degree will soon to find themselves (including me) in the same predicament:

> *If you are not able to translate (no pun intended) business needs into software, albeit, using hand-written code, or orchestrating coding agents.*

 you find yourself replaced by coding agents that can do it.

Here is a fun read I came across the other day, what I see is a not-too-long future:
